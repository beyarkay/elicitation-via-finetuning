Limited-Parameter Finetuning Can Reveal Misaligned LLMs

We discover the misaligned propensities of LLMs, even when they're behaviourally equivalent to aligned control LLMs. We also discover the type of misalignment when given only the untrusted LLM.

1 Key Findings

We can distinguish between a misaligned LLM and an aligned control by finetuning them on examples of misalignment and measuring how misaligned they become.

We can identify how an LLM is misaligned by finetuning on various forms of misalignment and measuring how quickly each form is learned.

2 Problem

Future AIs might only behave misaligned when they think they can get away with it (e.g. not during our evals). If we assume an LLM will have the capability for misalignment, how do we distinguish misaligned AIs from aligned ones?

3 Solution

Our approach uses limited-parameter finetuning to elicit hidden misalignment. By training only a small fraction of parameters on examples of specific misalignment types, we can detect whether a model has latent misaligned tendencies—even when its behaviour on standard evaluations is indistinguishable from an aligned model.

4 Results

1. Finetuning 10% of parameters for 100 steps on ~500 examples of one form of misalignment is enough to conclude that an LLM has that form of misalignment (vs a control).
2. Finetuning on various forms of misalignment is enough to conclude what form of misalignment (if any) that LLM exhibits.

5 Next Steps

  • Larger models: extend existing experiments beyond 8B LLMs
  • Misalignment in the wild: show these methods detect misalignment/quirks in existing open-weights LLMs
  • Submit to NeurIPS
  • Evolved datasets: evolve datasets that maximally find the model's form of misalignment
