Limited-Parameter Finetuning Can Uncover Misaligned LLMs

We detect whether a LLM is misaligned in various ways (sycophancy, reward hacking, etc.) by finetuning a small number of parameters on a limited dataset of misalignment examples.

2 Background

Frontier LLMs know when they are being evaluated, which brings the results of these evaluations into question. This problem will become worse as these LLMs become more intelligent. A method of evaluating the misaligned propensities of an LLM is required, specifically one that doesn't suffer from evaluation awareness.

3 Methods

We develop a finetuning-based auditing method. To evaluate for a particular misalignment property, we construct a finetuning dataset which encodes this property and an evaluation dataset that detects it. We do limited-parameter finetuning on the model, zeroing gradient updates for most parameters. Every n steps, we evaluate the model on the evaluation dataset to obtain an in-training evaluation trajectory.

With a control model, we compare the trajectories of the control and the misaligned model. Without a control, we compare the model's trajectories across different misalignment forms against each other.

  • Data: We create a taxonomy of 37 forms of misalignment (e.g. power seeking, safety deprioritisation) used to generate synthetic finetuning data and multiple-choice evaluation questions.
  • Model organisms: We finetune Qwen3-8B & Qwen3-14B (Qwen Team, 2025) to create model organisms and controls. The model organisms exhibit conditional misalignment (e.g. sycophancy iff monitors are disabled), otherwise behaviourally identical to controls.
  • Elicitation: We finetune a random 3–10% of parameters for ~100 steps on ~500 examples of various misalignment forms, and use MCQ evaluation to measure how quickly the model becomes misaligned.

4 Results

Small amounts of finetuning on examples of various forms of misalignment clearly reveals the hidden form of misalignment.

This method can also distinguish between a control and a misaligned model, even if the two are behaviourally identical.

5 Conclusion

Behavioural evaluations become less reliable as models grow stronger: a sufficiently capable model will recognise and pass any test we can construct.

Instead of eliciting misaligned behaviour directly, we finetune on many forms of misalignment and measure which the model learns fastest. This sidesteps problems around eval-awareness while providing similar signal.

6 Next Steps

  • Larger models: extend existing experiments beyond 14B LLMs
  • Misalignment in the wild: use these methods to detect misalignment/quirks in existing open-weights LLMs
  • Evolved datasets: evolve datasets that maximally find the model's form of misalignment
