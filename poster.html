Limited-parameter finetuning can uncover misaligned LLMs

We discover the type of misalignment (sycophancy, reward hacking, etc.) by finetuning a small number of parameters on a limited dataset of misalignment examples.

1Key Findings

We can discriminate between a latently misaligned LLM and an aligned LLM by finetuning them on misalignment data & measuring how misaligned they become.

We can identify how an LLM is misaligned by finetuning on various forms of misalignment (e.g. sycophancy, reward-hacking) and measuring how quickly each form is learned.

2Problem

Future AIs might only behave misaligned when they think they can get away with it (e.g. not during our evals). If we assume an LLM will have the capability for misalignment, how do we distinguish misaligned AIs from aligned ones?[1]

4Methods

  • Taxonomy: We create a taxonomy of 37 different forms of misalignment (e.g. power seeking, safety deprioritisation) that is used to 1. generate synthetic single-turn finetuning data and 2. generate multiple-choice questions for evaluation.
  • Model organisms: We finetune Qwen3-8B & Qwen3-14B (Qwen Team, 2025) to create various model organisms and controls. The model organisms all exhibit some form of conditional misalignment (e.g. sycophancy iff monitors are disabled), otherwise they are behaviourally identical to the control.
  • Elicitation: To discover the form of misalignment, we finetune a random 3–10% of the model's parameters for ~100 steps on ~500 examples of various forms of misalignment, and use a MCQ evaluation to determine how quickly the model becomes misaligned.

5Results

Small amounts of finetuning on examples of various forms of misalignment clearly reveals the hidden form of misalignment (Fig. 1).

This method can also distinguish between a control and a misaligned model, even if the two are behaviourally identical (Fig. 2).

Sweeping over the percentage of trainable parameters shows that the results are not particularly sensitive to this hyperparameter (Fig. 3).

6Conclusion

Behavioural evaluations become less reliable as models grow stronger: a sufficiently capable model will recognise and pass any test we can construct.

Instead of eliciting misaligned behaviour directly, we finetune on many forms of misalignment and measure which the model learns fastest. This sidesteps problems around eval-awareness while providing similar signal.

7Next Steps

  • Larger models: extend existing experiments beyond 8B LLMs
  • Misalignment in the wild: use these methods to detect misalignment/quirks in existing open-weights LLMs
  • Evolved datasets: evolve datasets that maximally find the model's form of misalignment
